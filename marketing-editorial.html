§ 01The lede

We have five senses for the world above the waterline. The engine has none of them for itself. It cannot feel its own bearings wearing, or hear the new note in the pump. cnrysense is the sixth sense it has always needed — a small, sealed device that bolts to its block, and an app that turns its running life into three quiet questions.

§ 02 · The problem

By the time the dials notice,
the damage is already done.

i.

The dials are the last to know.

Oil pressure, temperature, voltage — by the time a gauge swings, the fault is not developing, it has happened. The early shift lives below the threshold of any warning light on the binnacle.

— on detection
ii.

Traffic lights manufacture a crisis.

Green all week — then amber on Saturday morning, before the trip. You cancel the weekend, and nothing had actually changed overnight. Hard thresholds invent false cliffs that the engine itself did not cross.

— on false alarms
iii.

A tow home costs a weekend.

The cost of a breakdown at sea, even without drama, is measured in weekends. With drama, in far more than that. The sensor is, at its simplest, insurance — and very good company.

— on consequence
§ 03 · The sixth sense

Three channels,
no cliffs.

The five senses sit in the cockpit. The sixth lives on the engine block — and listens in three dimensions at once. Each channel drifts independently, and one often catches something before the others.

Ch. 01 · Heartbeat

Rhythm

How the engine feels

The vibration fingerprint, from a tri-axis accelerometer. Balance, smoothness, bearing condition — the shift that tells you something has started to wear, long before you feel it in the tiller.

Steady this week0.18 mm/s
Ch. 02 · What it says

Sound

How the engine sounds

The acoustic signature, from a MEMS microphone. Combustion, exhaust tone, belt noise — each has a voice, and when one begins to drift, its signature drifts first. We notice before it becomes a noise.

Warming slightly62 dB
Ch. 03 · Workload

Stress

How hard it is working

Temperature, load, usage patterns — is the engine working harder than it should, for what you're asking of it? One channel often catches something before the others. That is the point.

§ 04 · The app

No traffic lights.
No false cliffs.
Just the truth, gradually.

Most engine monitors sort health into green, amber, red — and manufacture a crisis every time a value drifts past an arbitrary line. We don't believe engines work that way. They age like a day fades into dusk: you cannot point to the moment it happened, but you always know where you are.

We call it the penumbra — one continuous gradient, one continuous language. It has no thresholds to cross, so there are no mornings you open the app to a colour you didn't expect.

The engine is new to us. We have listened, learned its fingerprint, and have nothing to say — which is the correct amount to say.
